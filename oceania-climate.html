Act V · The Risk Cascade

Four compounding threats, one region

No single event defines the crisis. It is the simultaneous convergence of multiple hazards — each worsening the others.

  • 01
    Ocean acidification at 0.4 pH units below pre-industrial levels

    Coral reefs that protect coastlines from wave energy are bleaching at catastrophic rates. Without reefs, storm surge damage increases by up to 5×.

  • 02
    Saltwater intrusion rendering freshwater aquifers undrinkable

    In Tuvalu and Kiribati, as much as 60% of fresh groundwater has been contaminated. Desalination plants now run on diesel — adding to emissions in a feedback loop.

  • 03
    Category 5 cyclone frequency doubled since 1990

    Warmer sea surface temperatures provide more energy to tropical cyclones. Fiji, Vanuatu, and the Solomons face a cycle of rebuild-and-destroy with no end in sight.

  • 04
    Food system collapse as crop yields fall 18% by 2040

    Shifting rainfall, saline soils, and intensifying heat stress are projected to reduce staple crop yields across all 13 nations simultaneously — a first in recorded history.
